# Streaming Platform Content Analysis

A comparative data analysis project exploring the libraries of major streaming services: Netflix, Disney+, and Amazon Prime Video.

---

## 📌 Overview

This project analyzes and compares content distributions across multiple streaming platforms. Using Python and data visualization libraries, it examines:

- Ratio of movies to TV shows  
- Geographical production trends  
- Historical content growth  

to provide a cross-platform perspective on the streaming landscape.

---

## 🚀 Features

- **Cross-Platform Comparison**  
  Aggregates data from Netflix, Disney+, and Amazon Prime Video into a unified dataset.

- **Content Distribution**  
  Visualizes the breakdown of Movies vs. TV Shows for each platform.

- **Geographic Insights**  
  Identifies leading content-producing countries (e.g., USA, India).

- **Trend Analysis**  
  Tracks library growth and release year patterns.

- **Data-Driven Insights**  
  Summarizes key takeaways regarding platform acquisition strategies and content types.

## 📊 Key Findings

- **Dominant Format:**  
  Movies are the primary content format across all platforms.

- **Library Scale:**  
  Netflix maintains a significantly larger content library compared to competitors.

- **Regional Leaders:**  
  The United States is the top content producer, followed by India.

- **Growth Trends:**  
  Netflix shows consistent growth in content over time.

- **Content Structure:**  
  TV shows often have multiple seasons, but their total count is lower than movies.

---

## ⚠️ Note on Data

Temporal analysis was primarily performed on Netflix data due to inconsistencies in date fields in other datasets.
